Salesforce · Data + AI · Two-Part Engagement

Delta Airlines

Customer Discovery, Integration Proposal & Predictive Loyalty Model · Oct–Dec 2024

Stakeholder discovery with Delta's Chief Data Officer and Head of Marketing, a Salesforce Data Cloud + Einstein + Agent Force integration proposal, and a Python logistic-regression loyalty model trained on cleaned multi-sheet flier data.

Chapter 1

Data Analyst — Loyalty Analytics & Predictive Model

Team 17 · 11 November 2024 · Python + Excel

Delta's fragmented data landscape — worsened by the 2024 CrowdStrike incident — was hampering customer issue resolution and strategic planning. The brief: centralise the loyalty dataset, surface behavioural patterns, and build a predictive model to anticipate churn.

Data cleaning & preparation

  • · Three raw sheets cleaned in Python (pandas) + Excel: Frequent Flier Raw (9,672 × 15), Flier Upcoming Flights Raw (15,078 × 15), 3-Month Upcoming Flights Raw
  • · Resolved inconsistent name formats, malformed dates, mistyped airport codes (e.g. ttt → TTT), inconsistent time zones (UTk → UTC), 10-currency misspellings, invalid flight numbers
  • · Removed 423 duplicates detected via Frequent Flier Number + Most Recent Flight #
  • · Standardised types: numeric IDs → strings, Y/N flags → Boolean, bag counts → integer

Descriptive analytics — what the data said

  • · Lounge usage ~49.8% — strong perk signal
  • · Top routes led by ORD → ATL, SEA → DFW, LAX → MIA
  • · Top departing airports: SEA (972), ORD (959), JFK (939); arrivals led by DEN (955), DFW (953), ATL (940)
  • · Inquiry mix: New Flight > Flight Status > Flight Deal > Cancel Flight
  • · 50.2% of flights delayed; 22.4% of frequent-flier flights delayed
  • · Upcoming-flight revenue mix: 53.7% flight cost · 29.3% hotel add-on · 17.0% car-rental add-on

Predictive model — logistic regression for loyalty

  • · Logistic chosen over linear: binary churn outcome, interpretable probability output, alignment with the retention business goal
  • · Feature importance: Additional_Service_Rate (top) > Avg_Checked_Bags > Avg_Addon_Spend > Lounge_Usage_Rate > Delay_Rate
  • · Covariance probe of Flight Delayed? vs Add-On Spend: slight negative but not statistically meaningful — flagged as a limitation, not a finding

Recommendations delivered

  • · Loyalty programmes that incentivise add-on spend + checked bags (the top-importance features)
  • · Active delay-minimisation programme that prioritises frequent fliers
  • · Dynamic pricing across seat class, with competitive benchmarking
  • · Promote ancillary revenue — car rental + hotel — already a material revenue contributor

Tools used

Python pandas Excel Google Colab Matplotlib / Seaborn Logistic Regression

From numbers to architecture

The analytics told us what. The next chapter answered how Delta should respond — with a Salesforce architecture.

Chapter 2

Salesforce Customer Discovery & Data + AI Integration Proposal

Stakeholder interviews · Solution architecture · Oct–Dec 2024

Following stakeholder interviews with Delta's Chief Data Officer and Head of Marketing, the engagement turned to mapping the operational problems uncovered and proposing a Salesforce-anchored architecture to solve them.

Discovery — what the stakeholders surfaced

Data Security & Management

Complex protocols but no unified data access. Weekly backup flagged as a streamlining bottleneck. Vulnerabilities exposed by the 2024 CrowdStrike incident.

Marketing & Personalisation

Salesforce Einstein + Agent Force under consideration but underused. Real-time personalisation limited. Disconnected Adobe Campaign, custom CRM, and IBS Software stacks.

Data Silos & Scalability

Fragmented departmental data prevents a unified customer view. Scaling concerns as customer volume grows.

Solution — four pillars

1 · Unify Data Systems

Consolidate all sources into Salesforce Data Cloud for a real-time unified customer view.

Eliminates departmental silos, accelerates operational decision-making, and gives Marketing and Customer Service the same customer profile in real time — so a service issue surfaces in a marketing campaign decision, and vice versa.

2 · Implement AI-Driven Automation

Salesforce Einstein + Agent Force for real-time personalisation and predictive analytics.

Powers automated workflows across service + marketing, scales personalised engagement without scaling headcount, and lets marketing campaigns adapt to live signals (delays, cancellations, loyalty tier changes) instead of running on stale batch data.

3 · Strengthen Data Security & Regulatory Compliance

Automated compliance monitoring for GDPR, CCPA, and PCI DSS.

Regular security audits, robust access controls (role-based + field-level), and data encryption at rest and in transit — a direct response to the post-CrowdStrike risk picture. Compliance checks built into the deployment pipeline rather than retro-fitted.

4 · Integrate Marketing Platforms

Streamline Adobe Campaign, custom CRM, and IBS Software under Salesforce.

Unified campaign management and targeting, data-driven segmentation, improved campaign effectiveness — and a single source of truth for customer engagement history that informs every downstream channel.

Expected outcomes

  • · Unified customer profile accessible across departments
  • · Enhanced personalisation across marketing + service channels
  • · Stronger security + ongoing regulatory adherence
  • · Streamlined operations: fewer manual tasks, faster execution

Risks & mitigations

  • · Adoption resistance → comprehensive training + change-management programme
  • · Migration downtime → phased rollout with pilot testing

Bonus angle

Sustainable customer engagement: Salesforce integration used to fold a carbon-offset option into the loyalty programme.
